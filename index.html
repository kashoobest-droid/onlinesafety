Staying Safe Online in Climate Education Spaces

Digital safety for youth & climate activists. Simple, friendly guidance for everyone.

Project Purpose: Individual cybersecurity student developing a youth-led digital safety education initiative supporting climate education spaces.

Why Digital Safety Matters

When we're learning about climate change or organizing action, we share ideas and personal info online. Digital safety protects:

  • Your privacy - control over your personal information
  • Your voice - keeping your accounts secure so you can keep speaking up
  • Your impact - preventing disruptions to your climate work
  • Your mental wellbeing - avoiding scams and harassment

Just like we protect our physical environment, we need to protect our digital spaces too.

Common Online Risks

Knowing what to look for is the first step to staying safe:

  • Phishing: Fake emails/messages pretending to be from trusted sources to steal login info
  • Fake Information: False climate data or misleading "solutions"
  • Account Hacking: Unauthorized access to your social media or email
  • Malware: Harmful software hidden in downloads or links
  • Impersonation: Fake accounts pretending to be climate organizations

Real-Life Examples

Example 1: "Hi! I'm from FridaysForFuture local chapter. Click here to vote on our next action!" (Link goes to fake login page)

Example 2: "Amazing climate solution! This simple device reduces CO2 by 90%!" (Too good to be true - often a scam)

Example 3: A "climate activist" DM asks for your personal story "for a documentary" then uses it to gain trust for requests.

Always verify before clicking or sharing personal information.

Password & Account Safety

Strong Passwords: Use 12+ characters with mix of letters, numbers, symbols. Avoid personal info.

Password Managers: Tools like Bitwarden (free) help create/store unique passwords for every account.

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Add an extra step like a code from your phone when logging in.

Regular Checkups: Review account security settings monthly. Check haveibeenpwned.com to see if your email appears in data breaches.

Social Media Safety

Privacy Settings: Review who can see your posts, tag you, or send you messages. Set to "Friends" or "Private" for personal accounts.

Separate Accounts: Consider having personal account (friends/family) and activist account (public climate work).

Location Sharing: Turn off unless necessary for an event. Be careful sharing real-time location at protests.

Harassment Response: Use block/report features. Document concerning messages. Don't engage with trolls.

How to Spot Misinformation

Check the Source: Is it from a reputable scientific organization or known climate institution?

Look for Dates: Old articles might not reflect current science.

Emotional Language: Be wary of content designed to make you angry or fearful without evidence.

Verify with Experts: Cross-check with NASA, IPCC, NOAA, or university climate science departments.

Reverse Image Search: Use tools like Google Lens to check if images are reused out of context.

If Something Goes Wrong

Account Hacked: Use "Forgot Password" immediately. Check connected apps/emails. Contact platform support.

Phishing Success: Change passwords on ALL accounts where you used similar passwords. Enable 2FA.

Harassment/Threats: Document everything. Report to platform and consider reporting to authorities if serious.

Data Breach: Change password for affected service and any similar passwords. Monitor accounts for suspicious activity.

Take a Break: Digital safety includes mental health. Step away if overwhelmed.

Resources & Simple Checklist

Quick Safety Checklist:

  • ✓ Strong, unique passwords + password manager
  • ✓ Two-factor authentication enabled
  • ✓ Privacy settings reviewed monthly
  • ✓ Think before clicking links or downloading
  • ✓ Verify info before sharing
  • ✓ Know how to report problems
